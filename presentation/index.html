Monte Carlo Simulation

You know how flipping a coin 10 times gives you roughly 5 heads?
Flip it a million times and you get the exact odds. That's Monte Carlo — but instead of coins, it's cars.

🚗

Drop 300,000 cars onto Toronto

Each is a simulated EV agent — like flipping 300K coins at once

📍

Each lands in a neighborhood

Weighted by real traffic data — 383K road edges, 260 postal zones

🕑

Each arrives at a different time

3.5M weekly trip legs — morning commute, midday office, evening rush

🔋

Each needs a different amount of power

Battery SoC modeled with Beta(6,2) init + sigmoid charge decisions

Semi-Markov Decision Layer

People aren't dice rolls — they make decisions.

Snow day? Work from home? Each life event reshapes the city's energy demand.

Under the Hood

Road Network

Real OSM graph: 145K nodes, 383K edges across 260 FSA zones. 11 highway speed classes + hourly traffic multipliers (AM +32%, PM +38%).

Mobility Engine

300K agents in 324s (927 ppl/sec). Weekly itineraries with 5 destination types. Purpose-driven: worker 62%, student 8%, retired 12%.

Battery & Charging

Beta(6,2) SoC init + sigmoid charge decision. 2,889 real AFDC/NREL chargers mapped. Winter penalty: up to 40% more demand at -15°C.

MILP Optimizer

PuLP Facility Location Problem with haversine coverage matrix. Prescribes charger type (L2/DCFC/Ultra-Fast), unit count, and BESS sizing per zone.

Validation & Scale

30+ Validation Gates

Every simulation run is checked against observed real-world data: Toronto turning-movement traffic counts (107 FSAs), IESO hourly load profile shapes, and AFDC/NREL public charger locations. Multi-seed reproducibility across seeds 101, 202, 303.
